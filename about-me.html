My journey into CS:

In high school, I was enamored with the idea of being a surgeon. When college application season rolled around, I made a decision to apply to as many combined BS/MD programs as I could. By the time everything was said and done, only one program - FAU's Med Direct Program - had accepted me. I was estactic and promptly committed.

Little did I know that I would soon regret this decision. It's hard to say what changed in between high school and my first year at college, but all I knew was that I lost that passion for medicine that I once had. Around the time when I was processing this realization, COVID hit the United States and I was sent home for the remainder of my freshman year.

I had a lot to think about a lot of things during this period of remote schooling. Away from campus, I was able to seriously entertain ideas of walking away from the BS/MD program and leaving everything behind - ideas that were once just distant fantasies. One day, it hit me that I loved living in these alternate realities and dreaded going back to what was my real life at the time.

Long story short, this period of reflection became the start of my journey to leave the BS/MD program and transfer out of FAU. Now, I'm in my last year at UChicago, doubling in computer science and math with broad interests in theoretical CS and a passion for game development.

If you've read this far, thanks so much! I'm really happy my story got the chance to reach you.
