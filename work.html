Tower Defense Style Browser Game

Game Developer Metcalf Intern | Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) Art Museum, Summer 2022

Languages/Tools used: Javascript, HTML, CSS
During the remote internship, I worked with one other student to adapt the museum's card game into a simple, educational browser game. The game concept is built around the art museum environment and takes inspiration from the tower defense subgenre: players are tasked with using artifacts as defender units to protect their base against enemies. Enemies are typical things which would damage pieces in an art museum, such as mold and camera flashes ("light monsters").

I learned a lot in the process of building this game alongside my teammate. From searching up how to open the developer console in Chrome, to making pixel art, to figuring out how to "make the mold walk on level 1", I discovered a love for game-making and its unqiue place right at the crossroads between creative and technical processes.

"River Game" (Simple Strategy Browser Game)

Passion Project

Languages/Tools used: Javascript, HTML, CSS
When I was younger, my dad would play a board game with me. The rules were simple: (1) Pieces can only move in the column they are originally in; (2) Pieces cannot hop over each other nor be stacked on top of each other; (3) On both players' first move, they cannot cross the "river", which runs horizontally across the center of the board; (4) A player loses when they have no more valid moves.

After numerous games which all ended in defeat, my dad told me that there was a deterministic way to win the game. He challenged me to figure out the trick; unfortunately, I never did, and he eventually told me. I decided to program this game with those secrets in mind, allowing the computer to get into winning positions when it sees the opportunity to do so. Of course, to make it fair for the human player, the computer will sometimes make a move at complete random without regard to how disadvantageous its position would become. Thus, it's possible to win against it, as long as you are careful with your moves.
